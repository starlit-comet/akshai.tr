PROJECT:DEVELOPMENT OF SIMULATION MODEL FOR THREE PHASE ELECTRIC LOCOMOTIVE AND ANALYSIS OF THE DC LINK OVER VOLTAGE AND MITIGATION”

The auxiliary converter is crucial in keeping three-phase induction motors used as trac- tion motors in electric trains running efficiently. The auxiliary converter’s intercon- nected components, including the rectifier, inverter, and DC link, assist control and cooling of the oil cooling blowers (OCBs), which are responsible for dissipating heat created during motor operation. This system’s vulnerability to supply voltage changes, rectifier breakdowns, or other disturbances, on the other hand, poses a considerable dan- ger. When the auxiliary converter fails, the OCB motor acts as a generator, returning energy to the DC connection and potentially producing overvoltage. This not only endangers the converter’s internal components, but it also activates a safety feature that shuts down the entire system, resulting in the deactivation of the induction motor’s cooling system. The resulting increase in motor temperature creates a considerable risk of locomotive damage. Addressing and mitigating the challenges associated with auxiliary converter disruptions is therefore critical to ensuring the relia- bility, longevity, and overall operational efficiency of electric locomotives, emphasizing the importance of robust design, monitoring systems, and protective measures in elec- trified rail transportation infrastructure.

Seminar: WIND POWER HYDROGEN PRODUCTION AND FUTURE OF HYDROGEN ENERGY

The world's most rapidly expanding source of renewable energy is wind power generation, which is expanding in an industrialized and scalable manner. The high scale manufacturing of hydrogen from wind energy has also been opened up by offshore wind power. Hydraulic electrolysis hydrogen production equipment is used to immediately convert the electric energy supplied by wind energy into hydrogen, and the hydrogen created by electrolytic water is easily stored for a long period. The specific procedures are: the production of wind energy, water electrolysis, hydrogen production, and oxygen production. Numerous industries, including transportation, industrial thermal processing, the chemical industry, and others, use hydrogen energy. Based on the distribution and growth of offshore wind energy's analysis this study uses the large-scale hydrogen (oxygen) industry as an example, builds the related industry chain, and suggests the implementation path for the large-scale development of the hydrogen (oxygen) industry under multi-energy cooperative power supply based on the analysis of the distribution and development of offshore wind energy resources. As Hydrogen made from wind energy - water electrolysis can be considered as the greener fuel which will not produce any harm to the environment and it will lead us to a more sustainable way of power.
